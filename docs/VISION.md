# Vision

## Product statement

Luna Marathon Atlas turns a runner's long-term activity and race history into a clear, trustworthy explanation of performance.

It should feel like a running journal that can reason: personal enough to preserve the story of each marathon, rigorous enough to test training hypotheses, and simple enough that every chart answers a real question.

## Guiding principles

1. **Every chart answers a question.** A visualization must explain a decision, comparison, or performance outcome.
2. **Personal evidence before generic advice.** Coaching claims should be tested against the athlete's own history.
3. **Transparent calculations.** Metrics must expose their inputs, assumptions, and uncertainty.
4. **Stories and numbers belong together.** Race memories, conditions, and context are part of the performance record.
5. **Start simple.** Prefer understandable statistics and comparisons before complex modeling.
6. **Private by default, reusable by design.** The first athlete is Jerry Luna, while the analytics architecture should eventually support others.

## Long-term experience

The app should help answer questions such as:

- Why did one marathon build outperform another?
- Which training patterns repeatedly precede strong races?
- How much volume, durability, specificity, and recovery does this athlete need?
- What should change during the current cycle?
- How confident should we be in a race prediction, and why?

## Success

The project succeeds when an insight is understandable, reproducible, and useful enough to influence a training or racing decision.
